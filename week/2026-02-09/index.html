Monday — Steak Fajita Rice Bowls

  • 3/4 lb flank or skirt steak, sliced thin
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced; 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt & pepper
  • 1 cup rice
  • Toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, salsa, avocado, lime

Sear steak strips 2-3 min over high heat, remove. Saute peppers and onions 5 min. Serve over rice with toppings.

  1. Start rice: Begin cooking rice according to package directions (about 20 min).
  2. Prep steak: Slice steak against the grain into thin strips. Season with cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Prep veggies: Slice bell pepper into strips. Slice onion into half-moons.
  4. Sear steak: Heat a large skillet or cast iron over high heat until smoking. Add a little oil, then steak strips. Sear 2-3 minutes, stirring once. Don't overcrowd — cook in batches if needed. Remove to a plate.
  5. Cook veggies: In the same pan, add peppers and onions. Cook 5 minutes until slightly charred and tender-crisp.
  6. Combine: Add steak back to pan with veggies. Toss briefly to combine.
  7. Assemble bowls: Divide rice among bowls. Top with fajita steak and veggies, cheese, sour cream, salsa, avocado, and a squeeze of lime.
  • Slice steak against the grain for tender bites
  • Get the pan very hot before adding steak — you want a sear, not steam
  • Let steak rest 5 minutes before slicing if cooking whole first

Tuesday — Chicken Burrito Bowls

  • 1 lb chicken breast, diced
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt & pepper
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained
  • 1/2 cup corn (frozen or canned)
  • 1/2 cup salsa, 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Toppings: shredded cheese, avocado, cilantro

Season and saute chicken 6-7 min. Serve over rice with beans, corn, and toppings.

  1. Start rice: Begin cooking rice according to package directions (about 20 min).
  2. Prep chicken: Dice chicken breast into bite-sized cubes. Season with cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook 6-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Prep toppings: While chicken cooks, drain and rinse black beans, warm corn, dice avocado, chop cilantro.
  5. Assemble bowls: Divide rice among bowls. Top with chicken, black beans, corn, salsa, sour cream, cheese, avocado, and cilantro.
  • Cut chicken into even-sized pieces for consistent cooking
  • For extra flavor, cook black beans with a little cumin
  • Add a squeeze of lime juice for brightness

Wednesday — Marinated Pork Tenderloin with Roasted Sweet Potato

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin
  • Marinade: 3 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp Dijon, 1 Tbsp tamari, 1/2 tsp lemon zest, 1 tsp each parsley & thyme, 1/2 tsp garlic, salt & pepper
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed, tossed with olive oil, salt, pepper

Marinate 1-8 hrs. Grill or saute to 145F (~15 min). Rest 5 min. Roast sweet potatoes at 400F for 25 min.

  1. Make marinade: Whisk together olive oil, brown sugar, Dijon, tamari, lemon zest, parsley, thyme, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate pork: Place tenderloin in a bag or dish. Pour marinade over. Refrigerate 1-8 hours (longer = more flavor).
  3. Prep sweet potatoes: Peel and cube sweet potatoes into 1-inch pieces. Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Roast potatoes: Spread on a baking sheet. Roast at 400F for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and tender.
  5. Cook pork: Remove pork from marinade. Grill over medium-high heat or pan-sear, turning occasionally, until internal temp reaches 145F (about 15-20 minutes total).
  6. Rest and slice: Let pork rest 5 minutes. Slice into medallions.
  7. Serve: Plate sliced pork with roasted sweet potatoes.
  • Don't skip the rest — it keeps the pork juicy
  • Use a meat thermometer for perfect doneness (145F)
  • Marinade works great on chicken too

Thursday — Lemon Herb Chicken with Roasted Asparagus

  • 3 boneless skinless chicken thighs (~1 lb)
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil, juice of 1 lemon, 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp garlic powder, salt & pepper
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed, tossed with olive oil, salt, pepper

Marinate chicken 30 min. Sear in skillet 5-6 min per side. Roast asparagus at 400F for 12 min.

  1. Marinate chicken: Mix olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Coat chicken thighs and let sit 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours in fridge).
  2. Prep asparagus: Trim woody ends from asparagus. Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast asparagus: Roast at 400F for 10-12 minutes until tender with slightly crispy tips.
  4. Sear chicken: Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add a little oil. Cook chicken 5-6 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through (internal temp 165F).
  5. Rest: Let chicken rest 3-5 minutes before serving.
  6. Serve: Plate chicken with roasted asparagus. Drizzle with any pan juices.
  • Pound chicken to even thickness for consistent cooking
  • Asparagus thickness varies — thin spears need less time
  • Add lemon zest to the marinade for extra brightness

Friday — Teriyaki Chicken Rice Bowls

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, sliced
  • Sauce: 3 Tbsp GF soy sauce/tamari, 1 Tbsp honey, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, 1 tsp cornstarch + 1 Tbsp water
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli or snap peas

Saute chicken 6 min, add sauce, cook until thickened. Serve over rice with veggies. Top with sesame seeds and green onion.

  1. Start rice: Cook rice according to package directions (about 20 min).
  2. Make sauce: Whisk together tamari, honey, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. In a separate small bowl, mix cornstarch with water to make a slurry.
  3. Prep chicken: Slice chicken thighs into thin strips.
  4. Cook chicken: Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat with a little oil. Add chicken strips, cook 5-6 minutes until browned and cooked through.
  5. Add sauce: Pour teriyaki sauce into pan. Add cornstarch slurry. Stir constantly until sauce thickens and coats the chicken, about 1-2 minutes.
  6. Steam veggies: Steam broccoli or snap peas 4-5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  7. Assemble bowls: Divide rice among bowls. Top with teriyaki chicken and steamed veggies. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
  • Slice chicken while partially frozen for easier thin cuts
  • Make cornstarch slurry right before using — it settles quickly
  • For crispier chicken, cook in batches and don't crowd the pan

Saturday — Crockpot Italian Sausage & Peppers over Rice

  • 1 lb Italian sausage links (GF — check label)
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced; 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt & pepper
  • 1 cup rice

Place sausage, peppers, onions, tomatoes, and seasoning in slow cooker. Cook LOW 6 hrs. Slice sausage. Serve over rice.

  1. Morning (10 min): Place Italian sausage links in slow cooker. Add sliced bell peppers and onion on top.
  2. Add sauce: Pour diced tomatoes (with juice) over everything. Sprinkle with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3 hours.
  4. Start rice: About 30 minutes before serving, cook rice according to package directions.
  5. Slice sausage: Remove sausage links and slice into rounds. Return to slow cooker and stir with peppers and sauce.
  6. Serve: Spoon sausage, peppers, and sauce over rice.
  • Brown sausage first for extra flavor (optional but worth it)
  • Use a mix of bell pepper colors for visual appeal
  • Great over pasta or crusty bread too

Sunday — GF Fettuccine Alfredo with Garlic Butter Broccoli

  • 9 oz GF fettuccine
  • 2 Tbsp butter, 1 cup heavy cream, 1 clove garlic minced
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan, salt & pepper
  • 2 cups broccoli florets

Melt butter, saute garlic 30 sec, add cream, simmer 3 min. Stir in Parmesan until smooth. Toss with cooked GF pasta. Steam or roast broccoli on the side.

  1. Boil water: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil for pasta.
  2. Prep broccoli: Cut broccoli into florets. Either steam (5 min) or roast at 425F with olive oil, salt, pepper (15 min).
  3. Cook pasta: Add GF fettuccine to boiling water. Cook according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
  4. Make sauce: While pasta cooks, melt butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic, saute 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in heavy cream, bring to a gentle simmer for 3 minutes.
  5. Finish sauce: Remove pan from heat. Stir in grated Parmesan until melted and smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Combine: Toss drained pasta with sauce. Add splash of pasta water if too thick. Serve immediately with broccoli on the side.
  • Don't boil the cream — keep it at a gentle simmer to prevent separating
  • Freshly grated Parmesan melts better than pre-shredded
  • GF pasta can get gummy — don't overcook, taste test early
