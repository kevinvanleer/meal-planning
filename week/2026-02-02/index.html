Monday — Crockpot Crack Chicken over Rice

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 oz ranch seasoning (half packet)
  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 4 slices bacon, cooked and chopped
  • 3/4 cup shredded cheddar
  • 1/4 cup sliced green onions
  • 1 cup white rice

Slow cooker LOW 7 hrs. Shred, stir, top with cheddar, melt 10 min. Serve over rice.

  1. Morning (5 min): Place chicken thighs in slow cooker in a single layer. Sprinkle ranch seasoning evenly over chicken. Cut cream cheese into cubes and place on top.
  2. Set and forget: Cover and cook on LOW for 6-7 hours (or HIGH for 3-4 hours).
  3. Before serving (10 min): Use two forks to shred chicken directly in the slow cooker. Stir to combine shredded chicken with the cream cheese sauce.
  4. Melt toppings: Sprinkle bacon and cheddar cheese over the top. Cover and let sit 5 minutes until cheese melts.
  5. Rice: Start rice 20-30 minutes before serving (or use instant rice).
  6. Serve: Spoon crack chicken over rice. Garnish with sliced green onions.
  • Cook bacon ahead of time or use pre-cooked bacon to save time
  • Chicken breasts work but thighs stay more tender
  • Leftovers reheat well — add a splash of milk if sauce thickens

Tuesday — Chicken Burrito Bowls

  • 1 lb chicken breast, diced
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt & pepper
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained
  • 1/2 cup corn (frozen or canned)
  • 1/2 cup salsa, 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Toppings: shredded cheese, avocado, cilantro

Season and saute chicken 6-7 min. Serve over rice with beans, corn, and toppings.

  1. Start rice: Begin cooking rice according to package directions (about 20 min).
  2. Prep chicken: Dice chicken breast into bite-sized cubes. Season with cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook 6-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Prep toppings: While chicken cooks, drain and rinse black beans, warm corn, dice avocado, chop cilantro.
  5. Assemble bowls: Divide rice among bowls. Top with chicken, black beans, corn, salsa, sour cream, cheese, avocado, and cilantro.
  • Cut chicken into even-sized pieces for consistent cooking
  • For extra flavor, cook black beans with a little cumin
  • Add a squeeze of lime juice for brightness

Wednesday — GF Fettuccine Alfredo with Broccoli

  • 9 oz GF fettuccine
  • 2 Tbsp butter, 1 cup heavy cream, 1 clove garlic minced
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan, salt & pepper
  • 2 cups broccoli florets

Melt butter, saute garlic 30 sec, add cream, simmer 3 min. Stir in Parmesan until smooth. Toss with cooked GF pasta. Steam or roast broccoli on the side.

  1. Boil water: Bring a large pot of salted water to boil for pasta.
  2. Prep broccoli: Cut broccoli into florets. Either steam (5 min) or roast at 425F with olive oil, salt, pepper (15 min).
  3. Cook pasta: Add GF fettuccine to boiling water. Cook according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
  4. Make sauce: While pasta cooks, melt butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic, saute 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in heavy cream, bring to a gentle simmer for 3 minutes.
  5. Finish sauce: Remove pan from heat. Stir in grated Parmesan until melted and smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Combine: Toss drained pasta with sauce. Add splash of pasta water if too thick. Serve immediately with broccoli on the side.
  • Don't boil the cream — keep it at a gentle simmer to prevent separating
  • Freshly grated Parmesan melts better than pre-shredded
  • GF pasta can get gummy — don't overcook, taste test early

Thursday — Honey Garlic Salmon with Steamed Green Beans

  • 3 salmon fillets (~5 oz each)
  • 2 Tbsp honey, 1 Tbsp tamari/GF soy sauce, 1 tsp minced garlic, 1 Tbsp butter
  • 12 oz green beans, salt & pepper

Sear salmon skin-side up 3 min, flip, add honey-garlic-tamari sauce, cook 3 more min basting. Steam green beans on the side.

  1. Prep salmon: Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper. Let sit at room temperature 10 min.
  2. Make sauce: Mix honey, tamari, and minced garlic in a small bowl.
  3. Prep green beans: Trim ends from green beans. Set up steamer basket over simmering water.
  4. Sear salmon: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a little oil. Place salmon skin-side up, sear 3 minutes until golden crust forms.
  5. Add sauce: Flip salmon (skin-side down now). Pour sauce around fish. Add butter. Cook 3-4 more minutes, spooning sauce over salmon repeatedly.
  6. Steam beans: Steam green beans 5-6 minutes until tender-crisp. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve: Plate salmon with green beans. Drizzle remaining pan sauce over fish.
  • Pat salmon very dry for the best sear
  • Don't move the fish while searing — let the crust develop
  • Salmon is done when it flakes easily with a fork

Friday — Crockpot Chicken Tortilla Soup

  • 1 lb chicken breast
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, 1 can (15 oz) black beans (drained), 1 cup corn
  • 1 cup chicken broth, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt
  • Toppings: corn tortilla chips, shredded cheese, sour cream, avocado, cilantro, lime

Place chicken, tomatoes, beans, corn, broth, and spices in slow cooker. Cook LOW 6-8 hrs. Shred chicken, stir. Serve with toppings.

  1. Morning (10 min): Add chicken breasts to slow cooker. Pour in diced tomatoes (with juice), drained black beans, and corn.
  2. Add liquids and seasoning: Pour in chicken broth. Add cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, and salt. Stir gently to combine.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 6-8 hours or HIGH for 3-4 hours.
  4. Shred chicken: Remove chicken breasts, shred with two forks, return to pot. Stir to combine.
  5. Prep toppings: Dice avocado, chop cilantro, cut lime into wedges, set out tortilla chips and other toppings.
  6. Serve: Ladle soup into bowls. Top with crushed tortilla chips, cheese, sour cream, avocado, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
  • For thicker soup, mash some of the beans before adding
  • Crush chips right before serving so they stay crunchy
  • Freezes well — store without toppings

Saturday — Crockpot Salsa Verde Chicken Tacos

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 cup salsa verde
  • 1 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt
  • Corn tortillas (GF)
  • Toppings: diced onion, cilantro, lime, shredded cheese, sour cream

Place chicken in slow cooker, pour salsa verde, season. Cook LOW 6 hrs. Shred and serve in corn tortillas.

  1. Morning (5 min): Place chicken breasts in slow cooker. Pour salsa verde over chicken. Sprinkle with cumin, garlic powder, and salt.
  2. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3 hours.
  3. Shred: Remove chicken and shred with two forks. Return to slow cooker and stir into the salsa verde sauce.
  4. Warm tortillas: Heat corn tortillas in a dry skillet 30 seconds per side, or wrap in damp paper towel and microwave 30 seconds.
  5. Prep toppings: Dice onion, chop cilantro, cut lime into wedges.
  6. Assemble tacos: Fill warmed tortillas with shredded chicken. Top with onion, cilantro, cheese, sour cream, and a squeeze of lime.
  • Double wrap corn tortillas for sturdier tacos
  • Chicken thighs also work well and stay very moist
  • Great for taco salad bowls too — serve over lettuce

Sunday — Teriyaki Chicken Rice Bowls

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, sliced
  • Sauce: 3 Tbsp GF soy sauce/tamari, 1 Tbsp honey, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, 1 tsp cornstarch + 1 Tbsp water
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli or snap peas

Saute chicken 6 min, add sauce, cook until thickened. Serve over rice with veggies. Top with sesame seeds and green onion.

  1. Start rice: Cook rice according to package directions (about 20 min).
  2. Make sauce: Whisk together tamari, honey, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. In a separate small bowl, mix cornstarch with water to make a slurry.
  3. Prep chicken: Slice chicken thighs into thin strips.
  4. Cook chicken: Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat with a little oil. Add chicken strips, cook 5-6 minutes until browned and cooked through.
  5. Add sauce: Pour teriyaki sauce into pan. Add cornstarch slurry. Stir constantly until sauce thickens and coats the chicken, about 1-2 minutes.
  6. Steam veggies: Steam broccoli or snap peas 4-5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  7. Assemble bowls: Divide rice among bowls. Top with teriyaki chicken and steamed veggies. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
  • Slice chicken while partially frozen for easier thin cuts
  • Make cornstarch slurry right before using — it settles quickly
  • For crispier chicken, cook in batches and don't crowd the pan
